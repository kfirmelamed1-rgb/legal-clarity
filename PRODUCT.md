# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-represented (pro se) family-law litigants in any U.S. state: parents in custody, support, property, protective-order, and appeal cases who cannot afford a lawyer. They are stressed, often on a phone or cheap laptop, working at a kitchen table or late at night before a hearing. Built by a parent who went through family court self-represented. Money is tight for these users; the tool is free and they bring their own API key.

## Product Purpose

One place where an unrepresented person gets everything representation would give them: honest advice grounded in their state's actual law (fetched by real web search from official sources), a three-sided comparison of their case against the other side against the law, a neutral virtual judge that analyzes the case the way the law requires, and a mock hearing / mock oral argument where a virtual judge questions them so the real courtroom is not their first time. Success = a user walks into court prepared, with verified citations, a clean record strategy, and rehearsed answers.

## Brand Personality

Honest, steady, prepared. Never flattering, never doom-saying. The voice of a careful practitioner who tells the truth about weak arguments because that is what actually helps. Not corporate, not legal-marketing, not activist; a well-run legal-aid desk.

## Anti-references

- Law-firm marketing sites (stock gavels, marble columns, "aggressive representation").
- Startup SaaS gradients and hero-metric templates.
- Anything that looks AI-generated or over-produced; judges and users both distrust it.
- Legal-tech products that promise outcomes ("win your case").

## Design Principles

- Clarity under stress: a frightened parent at midnight must be able to use every screen.
- Verify, never trust: every citation carries its official source; the UI reinforces verification, not authority worship.
- Honest over encouraging: the interface never dresses up a weak position.
- The prompts are the product: the UI stays out of the way of the analysis.
- Free means cheap to run: static single file, bring-your-own-key, no backend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Users under emotional stress, low-end devices, mobile-first realities. Spanish-speaking users are answered in Spanish by the AI; interpreter rights are surfaced. Reduced motion respected.
